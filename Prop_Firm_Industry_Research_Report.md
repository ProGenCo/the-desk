## 2. PROP FIRM INDUSTRY ANALYSIS

### Pass Rates and Payout Statistics

Based on a major study of 300,000+ accounts (FPFX Tech data) and multiple firm reports:

- **Challenge pass rate:** 5-10% industry average; Apex claims 15-20% first-attempt pass rate
- **Traders who get funded:** ~14% of all evaluation purchasers
- **Funded traders who receive at least one payout:** ~45% of funded traders
- **Overall: evaluation purchaser to payout:** ~7% (7 out of every 100 buyers ever get paid)
- **Average payout amount:** 4% of plan/account size
- **Earn2Trade pass rate:** 8.89-10.42%

**Translation for a 50K account buyer:** If you buy a $50K eval, there is roughly a 7% chance you will ever receive any money back. The average payout from a 50K account would be around $2,000.

### How Prop Firms Make Money

**Primary revenue: Evaluation and reset fees (dominant income source)**
- A firm charging $150/month for 50K evals with 10,000 monthly applicants generates $1.5M/month in fee revenue
- With only 5-10% passing, the vast majority of revenue comes from failed attempts
- Reset fees ($79-$299) create additional revenue from traders who want to try again

**Secondary revenue streams:**
- Monthly subscription/platform fees ($85/mo at Apex after funding)
- Activation fees ($130-$360 one-time)
- Data fees and platform licensing
- Profit splits (10-20% of winning trader profits)
- Some firms mirror successful trades into live markets for additional profit

**The core business reality:** Evaluation fees ARE the business model. Trading profits from successful traders are the marketing story, not the revenue driver. If too many traders succeed simultaneously, the firm can face cash flow problems.

### Industry Trends (2025-2026)

**Rules are getting STRICTER:**
- Over 40% of unregulated prop firms shut down in 2025
- 80-100 firms collapsed between 2023-2025
- CFTC considering CTA (Commodity Trading Advisor) classification for US prop firms
- Mandatory licensing expected in key jurisdictions by 2026
- Stricter KYC/AML requirements rolling out
- FIXML data submission standard required by June 3, 2026
- 95% of surviving 2025 firms had implemented compliance frameworks

**Competition is intensifying:**
- Payout splits trending toward 90/10 as the new standard (up from 80/20)
- Activation fees being eliminated by newer firms (MFFU, Tradeify Select, FundedNext)
- EOD drawdown becoming more common (replacing harsher real-time trailing)
- Daily payouts emerging (Take Profit Trader, Tradeify Select)
- Evaluation costs dropping due to competitive pressure

### Do Prop Firms Actually Pay?

**Firms with strong payout track records:**
- **Apex:** $598M+ in total payouts since 2022, $15.4M average monthly (late 2025), $56M in 90 days leading into 2026
- **Topstep:** Publicly tracks payouts, considered a pioneer in the industry
- **Tradeify, MFFU, Take Profit Trader:** Regularly cited as reliable payers

**Firms with payment issues/scam reports:**
- **Crypto Fund Trader:** Backdated rules to deny payouts at scale
- **Fidelcrest:** Retroactively changed rules, withheld payouts without appeal
- **True Forex Funds:** Correctly executed trades marked as violations, accounts canceled
- **My Forex Funds:** Froze payouts, applied new rules mid-phase, deleted funded accounts
- **Forex Prop Firm:** Ceased operations, website down, payouts never received (OSC warning issued April 2025)

## 3. WINNING STRATEGIES FROM PROFITABLE PROP FIRM TRADERS

### What Profitable Traders Do Differently

**Risk Management (the #1 differentiator):**
- Risk 0.25%-1% of account per trade (not 2%+)
- On a 50K account with $2,500 drawdown, that means risking $62.50-$250 per trade max
- ALWAYS use stop losses -- no exceptions
- Set a daily loss cap of 1-2% and stop trading when hit
- Stop after 2 consecutive losses (cool-down rule)

**"Secure and Stop" Methodology:**
- Widely used by profitable prop firm traders
- Hit your daily target, then STOP trading for the day
- Prevents giving back profits and helps meet consistency rules
- For funded accounts: reach buffer zone, extract payout, reduce risk
- Philosophy: prop firm trading is about extraction, not account growth

**Psychology Guardrails:**
- Stop trading after 2 consecutive losses
- If emotionally drained or distracted, pause entirely
- Treat each trading day as independent -- yesterday's P&L doesn't affect today's risk
- Never revenge trade to recover losses

### Account Scaling Strategies

**Multi-account approach:**
- Run multiple smaller accounts in parallel rather than one large account
- Apex allows up to 20 active accounts per trader
- Elite Trader Funding recently reduced to max 5 active accounts (was 20)
- Use trade copier software (Replikanto, TradersPost) to execute across accounts simultaneously

**Diversify across firms:**
- Different firms have different rule sets -- diversification reduces total breach risk
- If one account blows, others continue generating payouts
- Each firm's consistency rules differ, so spreading across firms smooths payout flow

**Copy trading supported at:**
- Apex Trader Funding
- My Funded Futures
- Phidias Propfirm
- BluSky Trading
- TickTick Trader

### Payout Optimization Tactics

1. **Extract early and often:** Don't let profits accumulate -- withdraw as soon as eligible
2. **Build the buffer first:** Get above the safety net/buffer zone before requesting payouts
3. **Manage consistency rule compliance:** Spread profits across trading days; avoid one monster day that locks you out of payouts
4. **Maximize step caps:** At Apex, each payout step has a cap -- request the full cap amount each time
5. **Triage accounts:** Focus effort on accounts closest to payout eligibility; don't spread attention too thin
6. **Use EOD drawdown firms for volatile instruments:** MES can spike intraday; EOD drawdown protects against being stopped out on a wick

## 5. RED FLAGS AND TRAPS

### Consistency Rules That Secretly Prevent Payouts

- **The 30-50% rule trap:** If you have one great day that represents >30-50% of your profits, you CANNOT withdraw until you dilute it with more trading days. This effectively forces you to keep trading (risking the account) to become payout-eligible.
- **Compounding consistency:** Some firms count ALL historical profits including prior withdrawals in the consistency calculation (Elite Trader Funding), making it progressively harder.
- **Hidden consistency in scaling:** Some firms remove consistency rules from evaluations but enforce them through scaling plan requirements -- a delayed enforcement.
- **Vague language:** Terms like "unusual trading patterns," "disproportionate daily profits," or "at our discretion" give firms the ability to deny payouts without clear rule violations.

### Hidden Fees

| Fee Type | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **Activation Fees** | Apex: $130-360; TPT: $130; Bulenox: $148; FXIFY Starter: $125 |
| **Monthly PA/Platform Fees** | Apex: $85/mo (Rithmic); varies by firm |
| **Data Fees** | CME real-time data often $15-30/mo extra |
| **Withdrawal Fees** | TPT: $50 for <$250; MFFU: $15 per bank transfer |
| **Reset Fees** | $79-$299 when you fail and want to restart |
| **Commission Costs** | Tradeify: $2.58 RT; varies by firm and platform |
| **Inactivity Fees** | Some firms require weekly trades to keep account active |

### Rule Changes After Funding

- **Topstep (July 2025):** Winning days from Express Funded no longer carry to Live -- changed the payout timeline for funded traders
- **Topstep (Jan 2026):** Moved to 90/10 split from 100% for new traders
- **Elite Trader Funding (Sept 2025):** Reduced max accounts from 20 to 5; reduced payout cycle maximums
- **Apex (March 2026):** Shifted to one-time activation fees from monthly
- **Industry-wide trend:** Rules are generally getting stricter, not looser

### "Too Good to Be True" Promos

- **Apex 80-90% off sales:** Run nearly constantly -- the "regular" price is the inflated price. The sale IS the price.
- **"100% profit split" claims:** Almost always have caps, step limits, or time restrictions
- **"No evaluation" instant funding:** Often comes with much tighter drawdown limits or higher fees
- **"Unlimited payouts":** Check for step caps, per-payout limits, and total account lifetime limits

### Payout Delays and Denials

**Common denial reasons:**
1. Consistency rule violation (single day >X% of profits)
2. Trading during restricted hours/events
3. Exceeding contract limits (even by 1 contract = instant DQ at TPT)
4. Account inactivity (not trading weekly)
5. "At discretion" clauses invoked for trading patterns the firm deems suspicious
6. Retroactive rule enforcement (banned at legitimate firms, common at scam firms)
